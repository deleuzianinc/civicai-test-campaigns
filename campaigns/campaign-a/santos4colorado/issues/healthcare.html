Healthcare Access

Lowering costs, protecting reproductive rights, and expanding mental health services

The Challenge

Healthcare costs are the #1 driver of personal bankruptcy in Colorado. Mental health services are scarce, with many communities lacking any access. And reproductive rights face constant threats from those who would take away personal healthcare decisions.

My Position

Healthcare is a right, not a privilege. I will fight to lower costs, protect reproductive freedom, and expand access to care for all Coloradans.

Key Priorities

  • Support Colorado Option public insurance plan — A public option creates competition that drives down costs for everyone, giving families more affordable choices.
  • Cap insulin at $50/month — Expand current law to cover more Coloradans. No one should have to ration life-saving medication because they can't afford it.
  • Codify abortion rights in state law — Protect reproductive healthcare from federal attacks. These decisions belong to patients and doctors, not politicians.
  • Expand Medicaid reimbursement rates — Ensure providers can afford to serve Medicaid patients, increasing access for working families who rely on this coverage.
  • Fund community health centers — Bring care to underserved areas where residents currently travel hours for basic services.
  • Create behavioral health crisis response teams — Mental health professionals, not just police, responding to mental health crises in our communities.

My Approach

No one should go bankrupt because they got sick. No one should ration insulin because they can't afford it. And no one should be told what healthcare decisions they can make for their own body.

I support a pragmatic approach: market-based reforms alongside public options. The Colorado Option creates competition that drives down prices. Expanded Medicaid keeps working families covered. Community health centers bring care where it's needed most.

On reproductive rights, I'm unequivocal: abortion is healthcare, and healthcare decisions belong to patients and doctors — not politicians. I'll fight to codify abortion rights in state law so they can never be taken away.

And on mental health: we're in a crisis, especially among young people. Schools need counselors. Communities need crisis response teams that can de-escalate without armed police. We need to treat mental health with the same urgency we treat physical health.

What It Means For You

If You're Struggling with Costs

I'll fight for a public option that gives you affordable choices, and for caps on prices for essential medications like insulin.

If You Need Mental Health Support

I'll fight for expanded services, school counselors, and crisis response alternatives that treat mental health with the urgency it deserves.

If You Value Reproductive Freedom

I'll fight to protect your right to make your own healthcare decisions. Full stop.
