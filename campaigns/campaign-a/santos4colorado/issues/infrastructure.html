Infrastructure & Transportation

Investing in modern infrastructure that connects communities

The Challenge

District 42 residents spend too much time stuck in traffic, and many lack reliable transit options to reach jobs, healthcare, and schools. Roads need maintenance. Pedestrian infrastructure is lacking. Working-class communities often get left behind when infrastructure dollars are allocated.

My Position

I will fight for infrastructure investment that creates jobs today and improves mobility tomorrow — with transit equity for all communities, not just wealthy areas.

Key Priorities

  • Support RTD expansion to underserved Aurora corridors — Transit for everyone, not just wealthy areas. East Aurora deserves the same service as downtown.
  • Fund road maintenance backlog — Fix what we have before building new. Our roads and bridges need repair, and that means good construction jobs.
  • Invest in pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure — Safe streets for all users. Not everyone has a car, and no one should risk their life walking to work.
  • Expand EV charging network along I-70 corridor — Support clean transportation. Colorado's highways need infrastructure for the vehicles of tomorrow.
  • Affordable transit passes for low-income residents — Cost shouldn't be a barrier to mobility. If you need the bus to get to work, you shouldn't have to choose between fare and food.
  • Prioritize projects that reduce air pollution — Infrastructure that protects health. Transportation investments should improve air quality, not worsen it.

My Approach

Infrastructure is economic development. Construction jobs today, improved productivity tomorrow, and a better quality of life for everyone.

But infrastructure investment hasn't always been fair. Wealthy communities get great transit service while working-class neighborhoods get passed over. That's not right.

Transit equity means ensuring that everyone — not just those who can afford it — has access to reliable transportation. It means affordable passes so cost isn't a barrier. It means service to underserved corridors, not just downtown routes.

And it means fixing what we have. Our roads need maintenance. Our bridges need repair. Before we build new mega-projects, let's take care of the infrastructure we already have.

What It Means For You

If You Commute

I'll fight for transit options that get you where you need to go, reliably and affordably, without spending hours in traffic.

If You Drive

I'll fight for road maintenance and reduced congestion. No more potholes that damage your car and waste your time.

If You Walk or Bike

I'll fight for safe infrastructure that protects all road users, not just those in cars.
