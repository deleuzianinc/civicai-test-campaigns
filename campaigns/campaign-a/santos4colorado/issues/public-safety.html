Public Safety & Criminal Justice

Investing in community safety and reforming a broken system

The Challenge

Our criminal justice system too often fails victims and over-punishes minor offenses. Meanwhile, gun violence devastates communities. We need a smarter approach to public safety — one that actually prevents crime, not just responds to it.

My Position

I believe in both safety AND justice. I will fight for community-based safety strategies and criminal justice reforms that actually work.

Key Priorities

  • Fund violence intervention programs — Evidence-based approaches that reduce gun violence by interrupting cycles of retaliation before they escalate.
  • Support independent police oversight boards — Accountability builds trust. Communities need a voice in policing practices.
  • Expand mental health crisis response — Not every crisis needs an armed response. Trained mental health professionals can de-escalate safely.
  • Reform cash bail — End wealth-based detention. Your bank account shouldn't determine whether you wait for trial in jail or at home.
  • Increase victim services funding — Support survivors, not just punishment. Victims deserve resources for healing and recovery.
  • Reform sentences for nonviolent drug offenses — Treatment, not incarceration. Addiction is a health issue, not a crime.

My Approach

This issue is personal. My brother struggled with addiction for years. He cycled through an incarceration system that did nothing to help him recover — it just trapped him in a loop of arrest, jail, release, relapse. Today, he's 8 years sober and works as a peer counselor helping others. He got better despite the system, not because of it.

I believe in public safety — real safety, not just tough talk. That means investing in prevention, intervention, and opportunity. It means police who are accountable to the communities they serve. It means mental health crisis teams that can de-escalate without guns.

I don't support defunding police. But I do support reimagining public safety to include more than just armed response. When someone is having a mental health crisis, we need trained professionals, not just officers. When violence threatens a neighborhood, we need intervention programs that actually work.

And victims deserve better too. More funding for survivor services. More support for healing. A system that centers justice for those who've been harmed.

What It Means For You

If You Want Safer Communities

I'll fight for evidence-based approaches that actually reduce violence, not just rhetoric that sounds tough but doesn't work.

If You Want Accountability

I'll fight for independent oversight and police reforms that build trust between law enforcement and the communities they serve.

If You've Been Impacted by the System

I'll fight for reforms that treat people with dignity and focus on healing — for victims and for those who've made mistakes.
