Immigration

Welcoming immigrants and protecting families from discrimination

The Challenge

Immigrant families live in fear of federal overreach. Local resources are strained by the federal government's failure to act. And too many politicians scapegoat immigrants instead of solving problems. Meanwhile, immigrant communities contribute to our economy, our culture, and our communities every day.

My Position

Colorado should welcome immigrants and protect immigrant communities. Local police should focus on public safety, not federal immigration enforcement.

Key Priorities

  • Maintain Colorado's sanctuary policies — Local police focus on local public safety. When immigrants fear police, they don't report crimes, and everyone is less safe.
  • Expand state-funded legal aid — Help immigrants facing deportation navigate the system. Everyone deserves due process.
  • Support driver's licenses for all residents — Safety on the roads, regardless of status. Licensed drivers are insured drivers.
  • Protect DACA recipients — Advocate for federal pathway to citizenship for Dreamers who know no other home.
  • Fund immigrant integration programs — English, civics, job training. Help newcomers succeed and contribute.
  • Oppose employer exploitation — Protect undocumented workers from wage theft and unsafe conditions. Exploitation hurts all workers.

My Approach

I'm the daughter of immigrants. My parents came to Colorado seeking opportunity, and they found it. They worked hard, paid taxes, raised a family, and contributed to their community for 40 years.

That's the immigrant story. Not the fear-mongering you hear from politicians looking for scapegoats.

I'll fight to protect immigrant families from discrimination and deportation. Local police should focus on public safety — keeping all residents safe — not doing the federal government's enforcement work. Sanctuary policies make communities safer because they mean immigrants will report crimes and cooperate with police.

And I'll advocate for comprehensive federal immigration reform. Congress has failed to act for decades. We need a real pathway to citizenship for people who've been contributing to our communities for years.

What It Means For You

If You're an Immigrant

I'll fight for your protection, your dignity, and your place in this community. You belong here.

If You're a DACA Recipient

I'll fight for your security and advocate for a permanent solution. This is your home.

If You Believe in Fairness

I'll fight against scapegoating and for policies based on facts, not fear.
