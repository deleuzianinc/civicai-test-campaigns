Meet Maria

Lifelong Coloradan. Housing Advocate. Mom. Fighter for Working Families.

My Story

I grew up in Aurora, the daughter of Mexican immigrants who came to Colorado searching for opportunity. My father worked as a carpenter for 35 years. My mother was a home health aide. They worked multiple jobs, long hours, and sacrificed everything to give me and my siblings a chance at a better life.

We didn't have much, but we had community. Our neighbors looked out for each other. When someone struggled, we showed up. That's the Colorado I grew up in ‚Äî and it's the Colorado I'm fighting to protect.

I was the first person in my family to go to college. I earned my degree in Public Administration from Metropolitan State University Denver in 2000, and later my Master's in Public Policy from the University of Colorado Denver in 2008. Education changed my life, and I've spent my career working to make sure others have the same opportunities.

Today, I live in the same Aurora neighborhood where I grew up, in a home my husband David and I purchased in 2006. David has taught high school for 22 years in Aurora Public Schools. Our daughter Sofia is studying environmental science at CU Boulder. Our son Marco is a junior at Rangeview High School, where he plays on the soccer team.

I'm running for State Representative because I believe in this community. I believe in our future. And I know that with the right leadership, we can build a Colorado where everyone ‚Äî not just the wealthy ‚Äî can thrive.
