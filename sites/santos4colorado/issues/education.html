Education

Fully funding public schools and supporting the teachers who shape our future

The Challenge

Colorado ranks 39th nationally in per-pupil funding. Starting teacher salaries can't compete with neighboring states. Our schools are asked to do more with less while students face increasing challenges — from mental health crises to learning gaps from the pandemic.

My Position

I will fight for full funding of public schools, fair pay for teachers, and ensuring every student has access to quality education regardless of ZIP code.

Key Priorities

  • Support full funding of School Finance Act amendments — Close the funding gap that shortchanges our kids. Colorado owes its schools billions in delayed funding.
  • Increase starting teacher salary to $50,000 statewide — Recruit and retain the best educators by paying them what they're worth. Teachers shouldn't need second jobs.
  • Expand universal pre-K to all 3-year-olds — Give every child a strong start. Early education pays dividends for a lifetime.
  • Fund mental health counselors at 1:250 student ratio — Our kids are struggling. They need support, and schools need resources to provide it.
  • Oppose voucher programs — Keep public dollars in public schools. Private school vouchers drain resources from the schools that serve the most students.
  • Invest in career and technical education (CTE) — College isn't the only pathway to success. CTE programs prepare students for good-paying careers.

My Approach

This issue is personal. My kids — Sofia and Marco — attended Aurora Public Schools. My husband David has taught in APS for 22 years. I've seen the challenges from inside the system: overcrowded classrooms, burned-out teachers, students who need more support than the system can provide.

Education changed my life. I was a first-generation college student. Without public schools that believed in me, I wouldn't be where I am today. Every child deserves that same opportunity.

Colorado has fallen behind, and our kids are paying the price. We've underinvested for decades, and teachers are leaving for states that pay better. This isn't sustainable.

I'll fight to move Colorado from 39th to the top 25 in per-pupil funding within five years. This requires sustainable revenue solutions and efficient resource allocation — not just promises, but a real plan with real accountability.

What It Means For You

If You're a Parent

Your child deserves a great education, no matter which school they attend. I'll fight for the funding and support to make that happen in every classroom.

If You're a Teacher

You deserve fair pay and respect for the critical work you do. I'll fight for salaries that reflect your value and working conditions that let you do your job.

If You're a Student

You deserve support — academic, emotional, and practical. I'll fight for mental health services, career pathways, and schools that see your potential.
