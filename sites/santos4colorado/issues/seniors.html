Seniors & Aging

Ensuring Colorado seniors can age with dignity

The Challenge

Seniors on fixed incomes can't keep pace with rising property taxes and rents. Many lack access to quality in-home care. Too many face isolation, and elder abuse remains an underreported crisis. Our seniors built this community — they deserve better.

My Position

I will fight to ensure that seniors who built this community can remain here, in their homes, with the support they need to live independently and with dignity.

Key Priorities

  • Expand property tax relief for seniors on fixed incomes — Stay in the home you worked for. Rising property taxes shouldn't force you out after decades of ownership.
  • Fund senior center expansion and programming — Combat isolation with community. Senior centers are lifelines, not luxuries.
  • Support in-home care alternatives — Age at home, not in institutions. Most seniors want to stay home, and we should help them do it.
  • Strengthen elder abuse prevention and prosecution — Protect the vulnerable. Elder abuse is a hidden crisis that demands attention.
  • Protect senior housing from market-rate conversion — Keep affordable options available. When affordable senior housing disappears, seniors have nowhere to go.
  • Support intergenerational housing models — Innovation in housing solutions. New models can provide affordable housing for seniors while building community.

My Approach

My work in housing taught me that seniors are among the most vulnerable to displacement. You worked your whole life, paid off your home, planned for retirement — and then property taxes spike and you can't afford to stay. That's wrong.

Seniors deserve security. They deserve to age in their homes, in their communities, surrounded by the people they love. That means property tax relief so fixed incomes can keep pace. It means funding for in-home care so people can stay out of institutions. It means protecting existing senior housing from conversion to market-rate.

And it means community. Senior centers aren't just nice-to-haves — they're lifelines that combat isolation and keep people connected. We need to invest in programming that keeps seniors engaged, healthy, and part of community life.

What It Means For You

If You're Approaching Retirement

I'll fight for policies that protect your ability to stay in your home and community as you age.

If You're Caring for Aging Parents

I'll fight for support services and in-home care options that ease the burden on family caregivers.

If You're a Senior Now

I'll fight for your security, your dignity, and your voice in the decisions that affect you.
