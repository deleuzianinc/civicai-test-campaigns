Housing & Affordability

Expanding affordable housing and protecting renters from displacement

The Challenge

Colorado has lost over 60,000 affordable housing units in the past decade. Median rent in Aurora increased 45% since 2018 while wages grew only 12%. Working families are being pushed out of communities they've called home for generations.

My Position

I will fight to expand affordable housing supply and protect renters from displacement through strategic investment and policy reform.

Key Priorities

  • Increase state Low Income Housing Tax Credit allocation by 40% — Build more affordable units faster by expanding the state's most effective financing tool for affordable housing development.
  • Create Colorado Housing Trust Fund with $50M annual appropriation — Dedicated, sustainable funding for housing programs that doesn't depend on the budget battles of any given year.
  • Enact just-cause eviction protections statewide — No one should lose their home without legitimate reason. Tenants deserve basic security against arbitrary displacement.
  • Legalize accessory dwelling units (ADUs) in single-family zones — Create more housing options in existing neighborhoods, allowing homeowners to build granny flats and basement apartments.
  • Establish rental assistance emergency fund — Help families facing eviction stay housed through targeted emergency support, preventing the cascade of homelessness.
  • Incentivize employer-assisted housing through tax credits — Partner with businesses to house workers, creating public-private solutions to the workforce housing shortage.

My Approach

Housing is deeply personal to me. It's been my life's work.

At the Colorado Nonprofit Housing Coalition, I've spent six years fighting for policies that keep families housed. At the Aurora Housing Authority, I managed 1,200 affordable units and saw firsthand how stable housing transforms lives. As a community organizer at Mi Casa Resource Center, I helped immigrant families navigate the housing system.

I know this issue from every angle — policy, management, and the human side. And I know what works: invest in supply, protect tenants, and provide emergency support when families face crisis.

I oppose blanket rent control because evidence shows it can reduce housing supply over time. Instead, I support targeted tenant protections — like just-cause eviction — combined with aggressive investment in building new affordable units. We need both: protection for today and more homes for tomorrow.

What It Means For You

If You're a Renter

You deserve security. You shouldn't face eviction without cause or watch your rent spike 30% overnight. I'll fight for protections that keep you housed and give you peace of mind.

If You're a Homeowner

You've invested in this community. I'll protect your investment while supporting responsible growth that maintains neighborhood character and increases property values.

If You're Priced Out

You shouldn't have to leave the community where you work, worship, and raise your family. I'll fight for affordable options so you can stay and thrive.
