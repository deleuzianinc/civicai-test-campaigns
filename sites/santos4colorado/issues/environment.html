Environment & Climate

Accelerating clean energy while protecting workers and communities

The Challenge

Climate change is real and urgent. Colorado faces wildfires, drought, and extreme weather. The Front Range has dangerous ozone levels affecting public health. But the transition to clean energy must be just — we can't abandon workers and communities built around traditional energy industries.

My Position

I will fight for aggressive climate action with a just transition — creating clean energy jobs while supporting displaced workers and prioritizing frontline communities.

Key Priorities

  • Support 100% clean electricity by 2040 — Lead the nation in clean energy. Colorado can show what's possible when we commit to a sustainable future.
  • Expand electric vehicle tax credits and charging infrastructure — Make EVs accessible to more families and ensure the infrastructure exists to support them.
  • Protect public lands from drilling and mining expansion — Preserve Colorado's natural heritage for future generations.
  • Fund just transition programs — Real support for displaced fossil fuel workers including job training, wage replacement, and relocation assistance.
  • Strengthen Front Range air quality standards — Clean air for frontline communities. No more dangerous ozone days that keep kids indoors.
  • Support environmental justice legislation — Prioritize communities bearing pollution's burden. Environmental policy must center those most affected.

My Approach

I reject false choices. We can have a strong economy AND a healthy planet. We can transition to clean energy AND protect workers whose livelihoods depend on traditional industries.

Climate change is here. Wildfires have devastated Colorado communities. Drought threatens our water supply. The Front Range faces dangerous ozone levels. We need action — real policy that actually reduces emissions, not performative gestures.

But I also understand that transition is hard. Workers in coal country didn't create climate change, and they shouldn't bear the burden of solving it. Just transition means real retraining, real support, and real jobs in the new energy economy.

And environmental justice means recognizing that pollution doesn't affect everyone equally. Frontline communities — often low-income, often communities of color — bear the heaviest burden. They deserve a voice in environmental policy and priority in environmental investment.
